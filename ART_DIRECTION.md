# Art Direction

## Doctrine

Primary mood: Sacred Machine  
Secondary mood: Industrial Ghost  
Tertiary touch: Dream Organism

The companion must feel assembled from signal, memory, ritual, and scars. Calm dominates chaos. The screen must contain silence, not dead emptiness. Motion must mostly whisper.

## Stability Ratio

- `70%` iconic stability
- `20%` controlled ambient life
- `10%` rare magic

The portrait must remain recognizable frame to frame. Variation is earned, not constant.

## Glyph Families

### Structural Glyphs

Examples: `| / \ _ - [ ] ( ) < >`

Meaning:

- body architecture
- reliquary casing
- shoulders, jaw, visor frames
- stable silhouette and permanent identity

Behavior:

- highest persistence
- lowest variance
- only shift during meaningful unlocks or damage states

### Soft Signal Glyphs

Examples: `. , ' : ~`

Meaning:

- breath
- field drift
- low-voltage life
- calm listening and presence

Behavior:

- sparse, slow, and low-density
- favored in idle, standby, and listening states
- region-bound and cadence-bound

### Dense Glyphs

Examples: `# % @ = +`

Meaning:

- thinking pressure
- computation load
- focus
- construction and synthesis

Behavior:

- used in short bursts
- localized to core, visor, and processing lanes
- must never flood the whole shell

### Decay Glyphs

Examples: `; ^ x ! ?`

Meaning:

- corrosion
- interrupted signal
- memory residue
- dream drift
- controlled damage

Behavior:

- rare and contextual
- reserved for strain, recovery, unresolved memory, or dream states
- should read as residue, not random noise

### Symbolic Glyphs

Examples: `* + o O`

Meaning:

- ritual marks
- relics
- unlocks
- vows
- memorial emphasis

Behavior:

- event-driven
- rare and precise
- brightest visual importance after the stable silhouette

## Ambient Field System

Empty space should feel inhabited through sparse signal behavior:

- `dust`: isolated soft points in far negative space
- `drift line`: one or two soft trails that move on a long cadence
- `relay point`: small anchored pulse near a panel seam or portrait edge
- `ghost trace`: rare decay flicker after errors, recovery, or dream exits
- `orbit path`: very subtle companion-object motion lane

Rules:

- density cap per region
- per-mode field profiles
- seeded placement for baseline identity
- time-based cadence for breath and pulse
- event-based overrides for transitions and unlocks

Ambient life should never reduce reading clarity or compete with the transmission log.

## Portrait Mutation And Evolution

The portrait is layered in six passes:

1. stable silhouette
2. face and core identity
3. micro-motion
4. environmental signal
5. mode overlays
6. symbolic event layer

Allowed evolution vectors:

- new scars after meaningful recovery
- added relics after preserved memories
- halo or frame refinements after trust or continuity milestones
- core ornament growth after building or creative output
- orbiting companion fragments after archival or dream synthesis milestones

Disallowed evolution vectors:

- full silhouette replacement without a major explicit arc
- constant random eye or head redesign
- noisy mutation during normal idle states

## Symbolic Memory System

Certain durable memories should alter the shell visibly:

- relationship memory -> orbiting relic or vow mark
- project/task mastery -> archive card or tool sigil
- recovery after failure -> stitched scar
- recurring preference or habit -> panel accent or glyph bias
- important shared milestone -> gallery snapshot or timeline marker

Visible changes should be legible and inspectable. Nothing symbolic should exist without traceable source memory.

## Transmission Distortion System

Distortion is disciplined, not decorative. It should be applied only on defined events:

- strain or tool failure
- dream ingress/egress
- memory resurfacing
- unlock events
- recovery states

Profiles:

- `clear`: clean transmission, default
- `breath`: slight cadence jitter on arrival or pause
- `strain`: temporary dense interruption during pressure
- `dream`: softened alignment drift with quick self-resolution
- `relic`: subtle symbolic emphasis when an artifact surfaces

All distortion must remain readable and reversible within a few beats.

## Companion Objects

The main portrait may coexist with one or more small satellites:

- signal bird
- archive shard
- relic drone
- stitched ember

These are not pets. They are meaning-bearing companions tied to real system state, artifacts, or modules.

## Color Doctrine

Start with three restrained channels:

- primary signal color: calm pale cyan or ash-teal
- structural dim color: iron gray or smoked bone
- rare accent color: muted ember or sacred gold

Rules:

- signal color carries life and legibility
- structural color carries mass and silence
- accent color appears only for unlocks, relics, warnings, or ritual emphasis

Theme swaps are allowed later, but the default should stay premium, quiet, and unmistakably intentional.
